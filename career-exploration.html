Career Exploration

Career Exploration

“Not all those who wander are lost” — J.R.R. Tolkien

According to a Gallup survey for Strada, some college students, depending on their major, do not have the confidence to step into the workforce. Nearly half of these students did not utilize their colleges’ career service resources.

One of the first steps to career exploration is figuring out what you want to do for the rest of your life and build your skill set around that. Now that I have your attention, no — you do not need to have your career or job choice set in stone. One purpose of general education is to expose students to a variety of subjects. While daunting, it is highly beneficial to give yourself some sort of direction towards a career path. Your school’s career services office is a great resource to discuss potential careers and there are other ways to explore future job choices. Take a personality test and find what interests you, talk to other students, family members, and expose yourself to a variety of career and job options.

Taking these steps early can help you make more informed decisions throughout your college experience. Be proactive and resourceful. Don’t be a part of the 94% of students from the survey sponsored by Hult International Business School who reported having regrets about their degree. A majority of those students believe that their schools’ guidance programs and counselors share some responsibility for their degree choice.
