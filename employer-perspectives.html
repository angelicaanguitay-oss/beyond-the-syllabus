Hear it from employers

Employer Perspectives

After doing a deep dive into the topic, I realized that I didn’t want to just see statistics about how employers viewed colleges’ preparation of students transitioning into the workforce. I wanted to hear directly from employers and ask what advice they would give students.

I reached out to seven local hiring managers from different industries and interviewed them about their experiences hiring and working with recent graduates. I intentionally did not share my own opinions on the topic so as to not influence their responses.

Here’s what they had to say:

Semi Conductor Industry - Manager
“Colleges students how to take in information, but not that practical skills that are needed in the workplace.”

Healthcare Industry - Business Owner/Nurse (RN)
“Have them get experience in a real job setting, even clinicals are not enough to prepare students.”

Healthcare Industry - Respiratory Therapy Consultant
“Colleges talk about skills but not about attitudes…personality is heavily weighed over knowledge.”

Visual Communications Industry - HR Manager
“Colleges do a good job teaching technical knowledge and critical thinking, but they often don’t spend enough time preparing students for the realities of the workplace.”

Hospitality Industry - General Manager
“Get an entry-level job in the industry that you want to work in even before you graduate…you would better understand how the business operates and what each role contributes to the business.”

Marketing Industry - Graphic Design Manager
“College gives students knowledge and training of the tools they would use in the workplace. But I don’t think they give enough real life experiences.”

Automotive Industry - Body Shop Manager
“Get your foot in the door starting off in any position. It’s easier to move up in the company than coming in with no experience.”
